MEME is a research implementation of the [Logic-Oriented paradigm](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11918) for quantitative trading. Instead of predicting returns from raw numerical data, it extracts the **underlying reasoning** that drives market prices — transforming multimodal data (technicals, fundamentals, news) into structured investment arguments, clustering them into latent narratives, and ranking stocks by the historical profitability of their dominant stories.

## How it works

MEME runs a three-phase pipeline each trading day after market close:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Phase 1 — Argument extraction">
    A LangGraph agent graph fetches technical, fundamental, and news data for each ticker concurrently, then synthesizes it into structured **Investment Arguments**: a directional polarity (+1 bullish / −1 bearish), an analytical justification, and supporting evidence.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 2 — Mode identification">
    Arguments are embedded with OpenAI `text-embedding-3-small` and clustered into **K Thinking Modes** using a Gaussian Mixture Model. A Hungarian algorithm aligns today's modes with yesterday's to track narrative continuity across time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 3 — Portfolio construction">
    Real close-to-close returns are measured for each mode. An EMA filter smooths profitability over time. A composite signal — `Σ(GMM probability × polarity × EMA momentum)` — scores every ticker, and the top/bottom 20% form the **Long** and **Short** portfolios with equal weighting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key capabilities

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="LangGraph parallel agents" icon="diagram-project">
    Technical, fundamental, and news filtering agents run concurrently via fan-out/fan-in for efficient per-ticker processing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Gaussian Mixture Models" icon="chart-scatter">
    Soft clustering (K=15–20 components) captures overlapping market narratives — unlike hard k-means assignment.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Temporal alignment" icon="arrows-rotate">
    The Hungarian algorithm tracks narrative identity across consecutive days, preventing semantic drift in mode IDs.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dual long/short portfolio" icon="scale-balanced">
    Goes beyond long-only: the bottom 20% of narratives drives short positions, testing whether bearish logic destroys value.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Defensive cash mode" icon="shield">
    If no narratives have positive EMA momentum, the system holds cash rather than forcing positions into a hostile market.
  </Card>
  <Card title="SQLite persistence" icon="database">
    Every argument, centroid, EMA value, and portfolio position is stored incrementally — enabling multi-week momentum tracking.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  **Academic research tool** — MEME does not constitute financial advice or investment recommendations. It is designed to evaluate the MEME system presented in [arXiv:2602.11918](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11918) in real-time forward-testing mode.
</Note>
